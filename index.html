About


I am a designer passionate about user experience, and I strive to create interactions that are simple and enjoyable for the user. I often find myself thinking about experiences and how they could be improved in my everyday life, with physical products as well as websites. In an age where anyone can create a website, I believe it's even more important to thoroughly research what the user is looking for and design an interaction tailored to their needs. I recently graduated from Northeastern University with a degree in Computer Science and Interactive Media.

When not designing, I enjoy running, nordic skiing, singing, wasting time on Netflix, baking, and practicing my calligraphy.

Emmanuel Episcopal Church

Web Design

As a part of my co-op at Cleveland Design, one of my long-term projects was to upgrade old client sites from Drupal 6 to Drupal 7. While upgrading, I also redesigned the home page of the Emmanuel Episcopal Church site and made the entire site responsive. Check

UX/UI/Web Design

In Interaction Design, we spent a large part of the semester developing our own interactive application. The application had to be responsive and it had to track something. As a busy college student, I chose to create an application that would help track time in categories. My application, Check, is a to-do list where students can plan their days and see a chart of how their time is being spent. As someone who plans her days down to the minute sometimes, I really felt that this interface was something I needed and would use. The class took us through all the stages of designing an application. We had to consider the users, create personas and scenarios, wireframe our application and test it with real users.

#wave 3D Art Installation

3D Sculpture

The final project in 3D Foundations was to create an on-site installation in Northeastern's art building. My group chose to construct a 'social media wave'. We used Adobe Illustrator to create waves made out of hashtags and laser-cut the hashtags out to construct a literal wave that looks like it is breaking through the wall. The wave was installed in the art building, and a photograph of it was used as the cover for the College of Arts, Media, and Design's inaugural magazine.
