About

I am a fourth year at Northeastern University, studying Computer Science and Interactive Media. I am interested in working in UX and UI design, as well as web design and development. Otherwise I like running, nordic skiing, singing, wasting time on Netflix, and baking.

Interactive Media

I believe that successful design of interactive media is simple and clear. The user can interact with technology, as a webpage or in another form, with minimal instructions.

UX Design

I'm very interested in UX design. I enjoy organizing and storytelling, and I think that these naturally lead to UX design, in the creation of personas, scenarios, wireframes, and more. Neither computer science nor interactive media would be anything without the users, which is why I believe UX design is so crucial.

Computer Science

I am familiar with HTML, CSS, Javascript, Java, Processing, Python, and Racket. Interested in web design and development, as well as mobile application development.

Check

Interaction/Web Design

In Interaction Design, we spent a large part of the semester developing our own interactive application. The application had to be responsive and it had to track something. As a busy college student, I chose to create an application that would help track time in categories. My application, Check, is a to-do list where students can plan their days and see a chart of how their time is being spent. As someone who plans her days down to the minute sometimes, I really felt that this interface was something I needed and would use. The class took us through all the stages of designing an application. We had to consider the users, create personas and scenarios, wireframe our application and test it with real users.

3D Sculpture

The final project in 3D Foundations was to create an on-site installation in Northeastern's art building. My group chose to construct a 'social media wave'. We used Adobe Illustrator to create waves made out of hashtags and laser-cut the hashtags out to construct a literal wave. The wave was installed in the art building, and a photograph of it was used as the cover for the College of Arts, Media, and Design's inaugural magazine.

The Garden of the Forking Paths

Interactive Narrative

The Garden of the Forking Paths is a short story that explores the idea of nonlinear storylines. For the final project in Narrative Basics, we had to take this story and create an interactive web-based narrative about it. My group made a choose-your-own adventure story, where the user chooses a character from the story and has to choose the correct options to get to the correct end of the story.
Note: The only complete storyline is that of Capt. Richard Madden, as we only had to make one as an example.
